101TrespassingTo enter an area without authorization.Access can be obtained either by being let in, sneaking in. Any area the person does not have access as located on his card, or any area cordoned off. This is not to be confused with §204 , which the person obtained access through force. Counts are stacked based on individual sector access.3 MinutesPenalty is based on how access was obtained, what area was accessed.§204

102LarcenyTo take an item which is not of their property, or misuse property.Taking items from worksites they do not have access too, or items that are owned by others. Counts are for each item stolen. For items stolen that are dangerous or important to station wellbeing, see §205.1 MinutePenalty is based on the value of item.§205

103BatteryTo have unwanted physical contact with someone.Even when the interaction is not violent. Bumping into somebody is not battery, however, after being inexplicably not to touch somebody, and doing so anyways, is.3 MinutesUser discretion.

113Excessive ForceCruel and unusual punishment by officials.Cruelty is defined as using force when the suspect presents no further harm. Unusual punishment can be anything from torture to anything outside regular penalties. This only applies to heads and security forces.5 MinutesPenalty is based on the amount of damaged caused, physical or emotional.

206Sparking a manhuntTo resist an arrest successfully and evade officials.If they have successfully resisted arrest and you must contribute resources into finding them, it counts as a manhunt. 207AssaultTo cause injury to another employee.There are three degrees of assault, first, second and third degree. The first means that they had the intention to harm them. The second means that they were acting in such a way that had caused harm to them. Third degree assault means they were involved someway in assault, such as telling somebody to do it.20 MinutesPenalty is based on severity of injury. If injuries are life threatening, escalate to attempted murder, and so on.

301MurderTo kill another crewmember, directly or indirectly.Similar to §207, there are four degrees of murder. See below
42
301-1First DegreeTo kill another crew member willingly and premediated.They planned to kill the person, and did, willingly, on their own accord.1 HourUnless there was some justifiable reason beyond self defence, it will always be permanent brigging.
43
301-2Second DegreeTo kill another crew member through lack of safe conduct. They did not plan to kill the person, but they died anyways.30 MinutesMust be argued, assume first degree, detective is useful here.
44
301-3Third DegreeTo kill a crew member under emotional stressThey did not plan to kill the person, but were aggravated beforehand due to emotional stress. Also known as voluntary manslaughter.20 MinutesMust be argued, assume first degree, detective is useful here.
45
301-4Fourth DegreeTo help kill a crew member indirectly.They failed to prevent a murder either by initating, lack of action or helped plan the murder.40 MinutesComplict, they must demonstrate that they will not be a harm to the crew if they are to avoid permanent brigging.
46
301-5Attempted MurderTo attempt to kill a crew member.They intended to kill the crew member but failed. Only for first or fourth degree.45 MinutesDepends on who they tried to kill, the circumstances.
47
301-6Involuntary ManslaughterTo involuntarily kill a crew member.They did not intend to kill the crew member, and the crew member ended up dying due to their unsafe conduct.20 MinutesDepends on how recklessly endangering they were being.

302MutinyTo rally against the chain of command.To attempt to overthrow the current chain of command, for example, making the head of security the new captain without doing it through proper channels. See Vote of no Confidence for the lawful way of mutiny. This MUST be done if the chain of command is to be broken. This law applies to ALL involved. 30 MinutesDepends on their role in the mutiny.
